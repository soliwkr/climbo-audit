## Overview

These are real posts from the Climbo Accelerator Skool community (933 members, Nov 2025–Jul 2026). Names and situations are as posted. Use these as models, not scripts.

---

## 1. "4 clients in the most simplistic way" — Jan 13 2026

A member in the UK posted exactly what he did: posted in a local business Facebook group saying he had a solution to help businesses grow on Google through AI-powered reviews. He offered 14 days free, including a "review reactivation" where he'd send messages to their past customers. No fancy pitch. No cold calls.

He got 4 clients from a single Facebook post.

**Lesson:** Local Facebook groups are warm. Business owners already know they need reviews. A free trial offer in the right place converts without selling.

---

## 2. "Our first paying client" — Jan 22 2026

A member who signed up in May 2025 but spent months dragging their feet out of fear. She finally started by approaching a Thai restaurant for a free trial. Then she pitched a French bakery in Fort Lauderdale during her anniversary trip. Then a hibachi and sushi restaurant back home. Her strategy: target businesses where English isn't the first language, because those owners respond especially well to AI replies in their customers' native tongue.

Three trials turned into paying clients.

**Lesson:** Fear is the only thing stopping most people. The first "yes" from a free trial client is the hardest. The next two come faster.

---

## 3. "Fortune favours the brave" — Jan 30 2026

A member who had been going to the same pizza place for seven years. He finally introduced himself to the owner before Christmas. One evening, with help from another Climber's post, he set up an NFC and QR code stand before heading in. He didn't even have an active Climbo account yet. The owner was impressed on the spot.

**Lesson:** Your best first clients are people you already have a relationship with. The NFC stand is a physical demo — it shows instead of tells.

---

## 4. "2.5 months then BOOM" — Mar 13 2026

A member who focused on restaurants and cafés. He spent 2.5 months getting rejected — owners who'd been contacted by Google and other directories before and were tired of pitches. He started to doubt his niche. He kept going. Then in one week, he closed 5 restaurants on annual plans.

> "3.000 € in one week is pretty awesome."

**Lesson:** The pipeline builds slowly, then converts in batches. Staying in one niche (restaurants) means you get better at the pitch over time. The owners who say no in month one often come back.

---

## 5. "Landed first client and 3 demos booked" — Mar 2 2026

A member posted a single sentence: landed first client and has three demos booked. 32 comments of celebration followed. Sometimes the win is that simple — you close one, and the momentum is real.

**Lesson:** The first client is a milestone worth sharing. The community celebrates it because they've all been there. That energy is real fuel.

---

## 6. "From BirdEye to Climbo" — May 22 2026

Wayne Connell (one of the community's top contributors) landed a new client who was previously paying for BirdEye — one of the largest and oldest reputation management platforms. BirdEye has hundreds of thousands of clients and has been in the market for decades.

Giacomo posted about it directly in the community: "Every business currently on BirdEye can level up and join Climbo. Go get them."

**Lesson:** You are not only competing against "no one has heard of review management." You're winning deals away from established incumbents. Position Climbo as the modern replacement.

---

## 7. "Win #4 — Hat Trick" — Jun 19 2026

A member and his colleague landed their first customer with three locations. During the World Cup they called it a hat trick. One client relationship, three GBP connections, three times the monthly value.

**Lesson:** Multi-location clients multiply your revenue without multiplying your onboarding work. Ask every client how many locations they have before quoting.

---

## 8. "#1 on ChatGPT, day one" — Jul 2 2026 (pinned post)

A member's client had paid for a year in full but spent a month on website tweaks without connecting their GBP. When they finally connected everything in one morning, the results came immediately. Brand new business, zero reviews yet:

> "Number 1 on ChatGPT. Number 1 on Gemini. Number 6 on Google Maps (out of 20+)."

Just from connecting the GBP and activating the GEO Agent. No reviews yet.

**Lesson:** The GEO Agent starts working before you have reviews. Connecting GBP on day one is the single most important step. Don't wait for reviews to set up AI optimization.

---

## Key details / Tips

- Free trials are the most common conversion path — show value before asking for payment
- Multi-location clients are the best-value deals (one relationship, multiple fees)
- The first client is the hardest; the pattern repeats much faster afterward
- Sharing wins in the Skool community generates feedback and accountability
