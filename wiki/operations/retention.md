## Overview

A client who signed up and got onboarded is not a retained client yet. Retention happens when the client sees ongoing value, month after month. Most churn in reputation management happens in months 2-3, when the excitement of the first reviews fades.

Your job is to keep the value visible.

## Monthly report

Send a monthly report to every client. It does not need to be complex. At minimum include:

- **Reviews gained** this month (count and examples)
- **Average rating change** (before vs. now)
- **AI activity summary** (what the SEO and Social agents published)

Climbo has a printable report feature (released Mar 2026 — available in the client's dashboard). Use it. A PDF sent by email or WhatsApp on the same day each month signals professionalism.

If you use the [[features/google-sheets]] integration, share a live Google Sheet with the client so they can check their numbers anytime.

## The "wow moment" delivery

Do not wait for the monthly report to share good news. When a 5-star review comes in, screenshot it and send it directly to the client — WhatsApp is fine. The message is simple:

> "This came in this week for you."

One sentence. One screenshot. This is the most retention-effective thing you can do. It makes the invisible work visible in real time and it reminds the client why they are paying you.

## Upsell path

Start clients on the minimum and expand:

1. **Review requests only** — the base product
2. Add **Social Agent** — automated posts from their reviews
3. Add **GEO Agent** — AI search optimization (ChatGPT, Gemini citations)
4. Add **Mobile App** — white-labeled app for the client
5. Add **NFC cards** — physical review collection at the counter
6. Upgrade to **annual plan** — offered at a discount, locks in the relationship

Do not try to sell everything at once. Get them to see value from step 1, then introduce step 2 after a month.

## Warning signs a client is about to churn

- They stop opening your monthly reports (check email open rates if your tool allows it)
- They ask "how many reviews did I get?" instead of checking the dashboard — means they don't feel connected to the product
- They complain the pace is "too slow" — usually means you haven't reset expectations, or the reactivation campaign ran out of contacts
- They ask about pausing — treat this as a churn signal, not an administrative request; book a call immediately

## What to say when churn signs appear

Book a 15-minute call. Show them their progress chart. Reframe pace: "You've gone from 12 reviews to 34 in 90 days. That's a 3x increase. Google's algorithm responds to sustained growth, not spikes." Then propose the next step (add Social Agent, add NFC cards, import a new batch of customers).

## Key details / Tips

- The Skool community post "Onboarding is not optional" (26 comments) is essentially about retention — clients who get a proper setup stay longer
- Month 1 target is 10 reviews; month 3 target is 30 total; by month 6 most clients won't consider cancelling
- Referrals from retained clients are your best source of new clients — one Skool member shared that a long-term client brought a friend to a lunch meeting, which turned into a new client on the spot
