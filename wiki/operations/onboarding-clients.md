## Overview

Onboarding is not optional. A community post with 26 comments said it best: "The better you get your clients set up, the longer they stay." Every minute you invest in a proper first session pays back in lower churn and fewer support questions later.

Plan for one 30-minute screen share or in-person session with the client. You do most of the setup; they watch and confirm details.

## What you need from the client before you start

- **Google Business Profile access:** Either they add you as a Manager on their GBP, or they share their Google login with you temporarily. Manager access is preferred — go to GBP > Settings > Managers > Add.
- **Business logo** (PNG, transparent background preferred)
- **Customer list:** A CSV or spreadsheet with past customers (name + phone and/or email). Even 20-30 names is enough to start.
- **Preferred review request message:** A short message in their voice — you can draft it, they approve.

## Step-by-step setup

**Step 1 — Create the client in Climbo**
Go to your Climbo dashboard > Clients > New Client. Enter business name, address, and contact email.

**Step 2 — Connect their Google Business Profile (GBP)**
In the client's Climbo account, go to Integrations > Google. Authorize using their GBP credentials. Verify the correct location is linked (important for multi-location businesses).

**Step 3 — Set up the review request template**
Go to Settings > Review Requests. Customize the SMS and/or email template with the business name. Keep it short and personal. Example: "Hi [name], thanks for visiting [Business]. We'd love your feedback — it takes 30 seconds: [link]"

**Step 4 — Configure the AI agents**
- Enable the SEO Agent (always)
- Enable the Social Agent (always)
- Enable the GEO Agent (optional at start — include it if the client is on a higher plan)
If you've white-labeled the AI, rename the agents in the client's view.

**Step 5 — Import the past customer list**
Go to Contacts > Import. Upload the CSV. Climbo accepts name, phone, and/or email. The import limit is 100 per batch — for larger lists, do multiple batches.

**Step 6 — Send the first reactivation batch**
Start with 10–20 contacts. Send the review request manually so you can see what goes out. Check that links work. This is also your proof-of-concept: reviews coming in within 24 hours is a strong wow moment.

**Step 7 — Show the client the dashboard**
Walk them through: where reviews appear, what the agents are doing, how to check their rating. Set expectations: "You'll start getting reviews within a few days. Month 1 goal is 10 new reviews."

**Step 8 — Month 1 goal**
Aim for 10+ new reviews in the first 30 days. This is achievable from a reactivation campaign with a modest customer list. Hitting this target early builds client confidence and retention.

## Common mistakes

- Skipping GBP connection — without it, the SEO and GEO agents don't work and review links don't point to the right profile
- Importing cold contacts — only use real past customers (see [[compliance/gdpr-eu]])
- Sending the full list at once — start small so you can review quality and timing
- Not setting expectations — clients who expect 100 reviews in a week will churn at 20

## Key details / Tips

- Total setup time: ~30 minutes with a cooperative client
- Do the setup yourself rather than sending instructions — clients who set it up alone are slower to get results and more likely to cancel
- The first batch of reviews is your best sales material: screenshot them and send to the client with a message like "Look what came in this week"
