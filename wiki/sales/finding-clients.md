# Finding Clients

> **One-line summary:** Your ideal client is a local business with few reviews, a competitor beating them on Google, and an owner who already values online marketing.

## Overview

The best clients for reputation management share four characteristics. Finding them is straightforward once you know where to look. Giacomo's training uses the phrase "foot in the door" — your goal is not to sell immediately but to prove the system works first.

## The perfect target profile

Look for local businesses that have:
1. **Low review count** — fewer than 50 Google reviews is ideal; under 20 is excellent
2. **Low or stagnant rating** — 3.5–4.3 stars (not 4.8+ where the sale is harder)
3. **A competitor beating them** — the competitor in the next street has 5× more reviews
4. **Marketing mindset already** — they have a website, post on Instagram, have claimed their GBP

Avoid businesses that:
- Have never heard of Google reviews and show zero interest in digital marketing
- Already have 500+ reviews and a 4.9 rating (they don't feel the pain)
- Are too small to pay $100/month (micro-businesses with one employee)

## Finding them

### Method 1 — Google Maps research

Search `[service] in [your city]` on Google Maps. Look at the results on pages 2–3. These businesses are already trying to be found but are losing to competitors.

For each one, note:
- Review count
- Rating
- Last review date (if it was 6 months ago, their velocity is dead)
- Competitor's review count for comparison

Build a list of 30 targets per month.

### Method 2 — Local Facebook groups

One community member got 4 clients this way (38-comment Skool post):

> "1) Find a local business Facebook group for your area. 2) Post saying you've created a solution for local businesses that will help them grow on Google through the power of reviews, on autopilot with AI. 3) Offer all businesses that commented 14 days for free including a review reactivation where you cover the costs to send a campaign to their past customer list. 4) Around 10 businesses commented."

### Method 3 — Face-to-face visits

The highest-conversion method, especially for first clients. Giacomo recommends visiting one local business per day:

> "Visit one local business per day. Ask for 60 seconds to show their Google ranking versus their competitor."

You walk in, show them on your phone that their competitor has 300 reviews and they have 20, and ask: "Would you like me to get you 5 free reviews this week?" If yes, you have a free trial. No commitment for them.

### Method 4 — Climbo Prospecting Tool

Climbo has a built-in prospecting tool that identifies local businesses by category with low review counts. Check your agency dashboard for this feature.

### Method 5 — Competitor's clients

From the community (May 2026): a member landed a client from BirdEye. "Every business currently on BirdEye can level up and join Climbo." If you know a business is on a competitor tool, they already understand the value. You just need to show why yours is better.

## The outreach script

Face-to-face cold visit:

> "Hi, I'm [Name]. Quick question — did you know that when someone searches '[service] in [city]', Google is showing [Competitor] above you because of their review count? I can show you in 60 seconds. I'd like to offer to get you your next 5 reviews completely free this week. No commitment."

You are not selling yet. You are offering proof.

## Key details / Tips

- **Niche focus works.** One community member focused on restaurants for 2.5 months with no results, then closed 5 restaurants on annual plans in one week — €3,000. Persistence in a niche pays off.
- **Industries that respond best:** restaurants, dentists, beauty salons, gyms, law firms, plumbers, contractors — any business with direct customer contact and reputation at stake.
- **The free trial converts.** Once the owner's phone buzzes with "New 5-star review," the paid sale is effectively closed.
- **Tuesday–Thursday are best** for cold visits. Mondays owners are swamped; Fridays they leave early.
