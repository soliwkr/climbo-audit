# Demo Script — Pitching to a Local Business

> **One-line summary:** You are selling to a local business owner (restaurant, dentist, salon, etc.), not to another agency. Your pitch is about *their* growth on Google, not about yours.

## The frame

You are a marketing agency that helps local businesses get more Google reviews, rank higher on Maps, and be found by AI assistants like ChatGPT and Google AI Overviews.

You are **not** selling software. You are selling a result: *more visibility, more clients, more revenue*.

The tool you use under the hood (Climbo, white-labeled as your brand) is invisible to the business owner. They see your brand, not Climbo's.

---

## The 60-second cold open (face-to-face)

Walk in, introduce yourself, then:

> "Hi, I work with local businesses in [city] to help them rank higher on Google. Quick question — when someone searches '[their service] in [city]', do you know where you show up?
>
> [Pull out phone, show Google Maps results]
>
> Your competitor [Name] has 280 reviews. You have 34. Google's algorithm treats that as a signal of trust. That's why they're above you.
>
> I can get you your next 5 reviews this week, at zero cost to you. No contract, no commitment. If you see results, we can talk about continuing. Sound fair?"

You are offering **proof before payment**. The free trial is your close.

---

## The 10-minute demo (if they want to see more)

Once they say yes to the free trial, schedule a 10-minute onboarding:

### Step 1 — Show the problem (2 min)
- Open Google Maps, show their profile vs. the top competitor
- Show their last review date — if it was 6 months ago, their velocity is dead
- "Google sees no activity. That tells the algorithm you're less relevant."

### Step 2 — Show the solution (5 min)
- "I use a system that sends a simple text to your past customers. They tap a link, they leave a review. No app, no account needed."
- Demo the NFC card if you have one: "Or — this. A customer taps here with their phone and they're on your review page in 2 seconds."
- Show them the review request interface (use a demo client account)

### Step 3 — Show the bigger picture (3 min)
- "Every review we generate: our AI writes a reply optimized for Google search. That reply contains the keywords people search for — 'best [service] in [city]'."
- "The same review gets turned into a social post for your Google Business page automatically. More activity = higher ranking."
- "And once you have enough content, you start appearing in AI search — ChatGPT, Google AI Overview. When someone asks 'best dentist in Formia', your name comes up."

---

## The offer

Once the free trial gets them 3–5 reviews:

> "You've seen it works. Here's how we continue: for €[price]/month I manage everything — review requests, AI replies, social posts, and your Google ranking. You don't touch anything. You just get more clients."

Keep pricing simple. One monthly fee. No setup costs visible to them (you absorb those). The value you sell is the **result**, not the features.

---

## Key details / Tips

- **Never lead with "software."** Business owners don't care about software. They care about customers.
- **The competitor angle is the most powerful hook.** Show the gap, not the product.
- **NFC card = physical wow moment.** Hand it to them, let them tap their own phone. The review page opens in 2 seconds. That's the demo.
- **Free trial = lowest resistance entry.** You pay the balance costs (few cents per SMS) for the trial. It's the cost of acquiring a client.
- **Keep the AI framing simple:** "It automatically writes replies to your reviews and posts them on your Google page." Don't say "SEO agent" or "GEO agent."
- **Track the outcome, not the process.** Monthly report: "You got 23 new reviews this month. You moved from position 8 to position 4 on Google Maps."

---

## What you are NOT selling

- "A $3k/mo reputation management business" — that's the pitch Climbo uses to recruit agency owners like you. It has nothing to do with your client's reality.
- AI software, SaaS, dashboards, or technology
- A "white-label platform"

You are selling: **more Google reviews → higher ranking → more phone calls → more revenue**. Everything else is behind the curtain.
