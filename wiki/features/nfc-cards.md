## Overview

NFC (Near Field Communication) is the same technology that powers contactless payments. When a customer holds their phone near an NFC card or sticker, their phone automatically opens a webpage — in this case, your client's review link.

No app download needed. No QR code scan. No typing. Just tap and review.

For businesses where staff interacts closely with customers (restaurants, beauty salons, personal trainers, clinics), NFC is often the highest-converting review collection method.

## How it works

### What you need

- An NFC card, tag, sticker, or countertop stand (purchased separately — not included in Climbo)
- A device to write (encode) the review URL to the NFC tag
- The client's unique review link from Climbo

### Encoding the NFC tag

You write the client's Climbo review link onto the NFC tag using a free app like **NFC Tools** (iOS/Android):

1. Get the client's review link from Climbo (`Settings → Review Link`)
2. Open NFC Tools on your phone
3. Select "Write" → "Add a Record" → "URL/URI"
4. Paste the client's review link
5. Hold the phone over the NFC card/sticker — encoding takes 1 second
6. Test it: hold a customer's phone to the card and confirm the review page opens

### Placement

Physical options:
- **Countertop stand** — best for reception areas
- **Tabletop sticker** — good for restaurant tables
- **Business card** — can be handed directly to customers
- **Framed card near the exit** — catches customers as they leave

The community has successfully laminated NFC cards without losing functionality.

### Bulk encoding

One community post with 31 comments asked about bulk NFC encoding. The short answer: yes, you can pre-encode multiple cards in batches before a client visit, making setup faster. If you serve many clients, buying NFC tags in bulk (e.g., 100 at a time) significantly reduces the per-card cost.

## Key details / Tips

- **Both NFC and QR code simultaneously.** Climbo's kiosk page has a QR code printed on it. If you place an NFC stand, also laminate the QR code nearby — some phones or cases block NFC, so having both ensures no customer is left without an option.
- **Lamination is fine.** Multiple community members have confirmed that laminating the card does not affect NFC functionality.
- **iOS:** iPhones from iPhone 7 onward support NFC without any app. Android phones have had NFC for years. Coverage is effectively universal.
- **You can encode to the Climbo review link or directly to Google's review form.** Sending directly to Google skips the rating screen. This is simpler but means all customers (happy or not) go straight to Google. If your client has a great reputation, this is fine. If they're still building it, use the Climbo review link first.
- **NFC tags are cheap.** A 10-pack of plain NFC stickers (NTAG213 or NTAG215) costs under $5. Pre-encode several for each client.
