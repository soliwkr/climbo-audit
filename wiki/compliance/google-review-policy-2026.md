## Overview

In April 2026, Google updated its Google Business Profile (GBP) Terms of Service to explicitly ban **review gating** — the practice of pre-filtering customers before sending them to leave a Google review.

Review gating means: showing unhappy customers a private feedback form, and only directing happy customers to Google. Many agencies and competitor tools have been doing this for years. As of April 2026, it is a TOS violation and can result in **GBP suspension**.

Climbo does not gate. This is a competitive advantage.

## What changed

- **Banned:** Sending customers through a "happy/unhappy" filter before the review link
- **Banned:** Routing negative respondents to a private form while only positive ones reach Google
- **Allowed:** Asking all customers for reviews (universally, without pre-screening)
- **Allowed:** Suggesting wording to customers who choose to leave a review (Climbo's AI Review Suggestion does exactly this — it helps happy customers write better reviews, but it does not filter who receives the link)

## Climbo's position

Climbo sends the review request link to **all customers**. There is no happy/unhappy gate. The AI Review Suggestion feature (launched Feb 2026) helps customers who are already writing a review to craft a better one — it does not filter who gets asked.

This means you will occasionally collect 3-star reviews. That is correct behavior.

## What to tell clients

> "You will get all your reviews, including occasional 3-star ones. That's actually more trustworthy to Google and to potential customers. A business with 150 reviews averaging 4.3 stars looks more credible than one with 12 reviews at 5.0. We keep you safe and compliant."

Many competitors still gate. If a prospect says "another tool only sends happy customers to Google," that tool is now violating Google's TOS and putting the client's GBP at risk.
