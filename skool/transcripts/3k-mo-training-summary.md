## Business Model Explained

The model is a **white-label SaaS agency**: you resell Climbo's reputation management platform under your own brand (custom logo, colors, domain, font) to local businesses at a monthly recurring fee. The agency owner's only real job is sales and initial setup -- fulfillment is automated by the software. This is explicitly positioned against GoHighLevel, framing Climbo as simpler for both the agency and the end client.

Key differentiator vs. traditional agencies: this is **not** a done-for-you service. It is a software subscription that clients self-manage after initial setup. The agency owner does not provide manual services month-to-month.

## Sales Framework

### 1. Prospecting -- "Find the Perfect Target"
Identify local businesses with these four signals:
- **Low Google rating** (e.g. 4.2-4.3 stars, not 5.0)
- **Low review velocity** (last review was 3-6+ months ago)
- **Competitors winning** (e.g. dentist with 24 reviews while the competitor two blocks away has 350)
- **Already marketing-minded** (has a website, posts on Instagram/Facebook -- already values digital presence)

### 2. Outreach -- "Foot in the Door" Strategy
Face-to-face visits (NOT cold email or cold calling). Walk into the business and ask for 60 seconds:

> "Did you know that when we search for [dentist/vet/etc.] in [city], Google is hiding you because of your review count?"

Show them:
- Their Google ranking vs. competitors
- An auto-generated reputation report (one-click from Climbo)

### 3. Guarantee -- Free Trial Close
Do NOT pitch the paid service immediately. Instead, offer a risk-free guarantee:

> "I will set up the system for you this week and get your next five reviews for free."

No payment, no credit card. Just prove the system works. When five-star review notifications start hitting their phone, the sale is effectively closed.

### 4. Conversion -- Upsell to Paid Plan
After the free trial delivers results (typically ~2 weeks), come back and present the $100/month plan. Frame it as keeping these results forever, plus additional features:
- Automated review replies (AI)
- Review marketing (social sharing)
- Simple reporting dashboard
- Listing updates
- Up-to-date website with schema markup

### 5. Daily Mission
Visit 1 local business per day. Dedicate 5 days per month = 30 outreach visits minimum.

## Feature Positioning

The training redefines "reputation management" as six pillars (not just collecting Google reviews):

### 1. Generate Reviews + Reply
- Automated review collection via three methods (see launch plan below)
- AI "SEO Agent" auto-replies to all reviews with customizable tone, length, language, and keyword targeting
- Bulk reply to historical unreplied reviews with one click

### 2. Protect Ratings
- Compliant (non-gating) feedback funnel: customers who rate 1-3 stars are shown a private feedback form first, but still have the option to leave a public review
- Private feedbacks managed inside the platform

### 3. Track + Analyze Sentiment
- Centralized review inbox (Google, Yelp, other review sites)
- Monthly AI sentiment analysis: what is going well, areas to improve, action plan

### 4. Review Marketing
- AI "Social Agent" auto-shares five-star reviews as posts on Facebook, Instagram, Google Posts
- Auto-generated captions, customizable prompts
- Video testimonial collection through the review link

### 5. Listing Accuracy
- Direct Google Business Profile integration: edit name, description, hours, special hours, photos, services, attributes, social links -- all synced to Google automatically
- Post Google updates directly from the platform

### 6. Website + Schema Markup (SEO)
- Auto-generated website from GBP data (name, description, photos, services, reviews, hours, map, social links)
- Automatic local business schema markup (rich snippets) embedded
- 100% correspondence between GBP data, website content, and schema code -- updated automatically when GBP changes
- Custom domain support

### Reporting Dashboard
Four sections in the home view:
- **Review statistics** (reviews generated, rating change, gamified goals like "12 five-star reviews to reach 4.8")
- **Ranking + competitors** (local rank tracker, competitor analysis, review score ranking)
- **Google performance** (direction requests, maps impressions, search impressions, call clicks, website clicks, keyword discovery)
- **Sentiment analysis** (AI-generated monthly report)

### Integrations
- Google Business Profile (direct)
- Facebook / Instagram pages
- Email + SMS (built-in, no Twilio needed)
- WhatsApp Business (direct integration)
- Zapier (CRM sync -- e.g. Google Sheets trigger adds contacts automatically)
- Yelp and other review sites

## Step-by-Step Launch Plan (30-Day Framework)

### Week 1: Preparation
1. Sign up for Climbo white-label platform
2. Brand the platform (logo, colors, domain, font)
3. Create a target list of local businesses in your area matching the four qualification criteria

### Week 2-3: Outreach + Free Trials
4. Visit 1 local business per day face-to-face
5. Show their Google ranking vs. competitors (60-second pitch)
6. Offer "5 reviews for free" guarantee -- no payment required
7. For each accepted free trial, set up:
   - Connect their Google account + social media pages (~1 minute)
   - Customize the review link (logo, feedback text, review site destinations)
   - Choose and configure the right review collection process:
     - **Option A -- QR Code in Loco:** Print kiosk page with QR code, place in business (reception, counter), train staff to ask every customer to scan and leave feedback
     - **Option B -- Manual Quick Request:** After each service, client sends a WhatsApp/email/SMS review request to the customer using pre-built templates
     - **Option C -- Auto Send (CRM Integration):** Connect client's CRM (or Google Sheets) via Zapier; whenever a new contact/appointment is added, automatically send review request X minutes after the service date
   - Enable AI agents: SEO Agent (auto-reply to reviews) + Social Agent (auto-share reviews on social media)

### Week 3-4: Convert Free Trials to Paid
8. Once clients see five-star review notifications (the "aha moment"), return and present the full $100/month plan
9. Highlight the complete feature set beyond review collection: automated replies, social sharing, listing management, auto-generated website with schema markup, reporting dashboard

### Ongoing (Monthly)
10. Maintain 30 outreach visits per month
11. Build case studies from successful clients (before/after: review count, rating, Google Map Pack position)
12. Use case studies to close new prospects in the same or adjacent verticals

## Psychological Selling Levers (Why Business Owners "Can't Say No")

1. **Clarity** -- They instantly understand the problem (low reviews = low ranking) and the ROI
2. **Fear** -- They are terrified of one-star reviews destroying their reputation
3. **Ego** -- They want to outrank the competitor across the street
4. **Peace of mind** -- Knowing that a steady flow of positive reviews buries the occasional negative one
